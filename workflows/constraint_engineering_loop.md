## âš™ï¸ The 4-Step Engineering Cycle

### 1. Observe (Behavior Telemetry)
**Goal:** Monitor "Behavior Signals," not just grades.
**The Data:**
* Is there procrastination?
* Is he guessing randomly?
* Is the output too verbose?
* Is there emotional interference?
**Logic:** Grades are *lagging indicators*. Behavior telemetry is a *leading indicator*.

### 2. Abstract (The "Money Step")
**Goal:** Objectify "Parenting Anxiety" into "Engineering Problems."
**The Translation:**
* *Parent Reaction:* "He is lazy." $\rightarrow$ **Engineering Problem:** "Context Length Issue."
* *Parent Reaction:* "She writes nonsense." $\rightarrow$ **Engineering Problem:** "Output Constraint Failure."
* *Parent Reaction:* "Too much drama." $\rightarrow$ **Engineering Problem:** "Cognitive Noise Interference."
**Result:** You stop blaming the child and start debugging the system.

### 3. Encode (Configuration)
**Goal:** Compile the abstraction into AI Constraints.
**The Action:** Write the rules into the **Kernel** or **Mode Engine**.
* *Rule:* "Reject any answer longer than 3 sentences."
* *Rule:* "If student guesses, force a 5-minute timeout."
* *Rule:* "Zero tolerance for emotional language in STRICT mode."

### 4. Deploy (Production Environment)
**Goal:** Let the AI enforce the boundary.
**The Friction:**
* The AI refuses to answer until the format is correct.
* The AI blocks the "Comfort Path."
**Result:** The child is not "lectured" by a parent; they are "shaped" by the environment.
